Harriet Tubman was a woman during the civil war that helped slaves get away from the south to the north. She was a conductor on the underground railroad which has how slaves got from the south to the north. People will remember her for what she did to save some of the slaves during the civil war.

  • She made a daring escape from slavery when she was in her twenties
  • Tubman became an African American icon and an inspiration for their struggle for equality
  • Harriet Tubman guided at least 70 slaves to freedom
  • She worked as a Union scout and spy during the American Civil War
  • Tubman was the first woman to lead an armed assault during the American Civil War
